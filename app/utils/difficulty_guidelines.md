# Hiking Difficulty Guidelines (SAC Scale & Standard Metrics)

## T1: Hiking
- **Terrain:** Path well-defined, terrain flat or slightly sloped.
- **Requirements:** No special skills or equipment.
- **Metric Heuristic:** Distance < 10km, Elevation Gain < 300m.

## T2: Mountain Hiking
- **Terrain:** Continuous path, sometimes steep, fall hazard possible.
- **Requirements:** Sure-footedness, hiking boots recommended.
- **Metric Heuristic:** Distance 10-20km, Elevation Gain 300-800m.

## T3: Challenging Mountain Hiking
- **Terrain:** Path not always visible, exposed sections with cables/chains.
- **Requirements:** Good sure-footedness, boots, basic alpine experience.
- **Metric Heuristic:** Elevation Gain 800-1200m, steep gradients.

## T4: Alpine Hiking
- **Terrain:** No path, some easy climbing, exposed terrain, glaciers safely passable.
- **Requirements:** Alpine experience, ability to assess terrain.
- **Metric Heuristic:** Elevation Gain > 1200m, high altitude.

## Scoring Formula (Heuristic)
A common way to estimate energy effort is:
`Score = Distance (km) + (Elevation Gain (m) / 100) * 2`

- **Easy:** < 5
- **Moderate:** 5 - 12
- **Challenging:** 12 - 20
- **Hard:** 20 - 35
- **Expert:** > 35
